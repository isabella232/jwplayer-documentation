This article introduces the video upload & management capabilities of the JW Player dashboard, powered by JW Platform.

Uploading your content

Start the upload process by clicking the big Add Uploads button at the left side of the Content List page in the dashboard. Then, select your file (or files):

In terms of supported formats, we accept nearly any audio or video filetypes. This includes video in MP4, MOV, FLV, MPG, AVI and DV format, or audio in MP3, AAC, WAV, ASF, PCM, M4A or AIFF format. Notable exceptions include content that use the Apple Intermediate, Apple ProRes or GoToMeeting video codecs. We also don't support content that is encrypted with DRM. See our Transcodes Reference for more detail.

Tip: Do not close your browser while video upload is in progress— that will cancel the upload!

Editing Video Details & Setting Metadata

Every video uploaded to the JW Player Dashboard has its own details page:

On this page, you can:

  1. Edit video title and tags. Tags are used to segment your video library.
  2. Set a preview thumbnail, either by uploading your own image or selecting a frame from the video.
  3. Add metadata like description and custom properties (via the Metadata tab).
  4. Upload text tracks, like captions or chapters (via the Tracks tab). These uploads should be in WebVTT or SRT/DFXP format.
  5. Upload a new version of the video and grab direct links to your video transcodes (via the Sources tab). This is great for when the video is already live, and you do not want to change your embeds. (Please note that it usually takes a while after uploading before these files are ready).
  6. Grab embed codes to publish the video to your site.

Custom Video Properties

Using custom metadata can be very useful when keeping track of, or displaying, pertinent video information. For example, when loading episodes of a show, you can use this to track things like season number, episode number, featured actors, or production date. This information is then stored inside of a playlist object and can be utilized with JW Player's Playlist API.

Custom properties can be accessed by clicking on the METADATA tab within a video object's detail page.

The above variables will then be stored within a playlist as:

<jwplayer:keywords>trailer, superhero, movie</jwplayer:keywords>
<jwplayer:VideoNumber>0012</jwplayer:VideoNumber>

In addition to media organization, such playlist information can also be used as values for targeted ads with our VAST client. This method requires the use of custom ad tag variables, which are mentioned here.

Publishing Videos

Hitting the Embed button will open the Publish Mode tool at the top of the page:

Here, select which player you want to use to publish your video by selecting the "change" button, if you'd like. Next, copy/paste the resulting embed code into your own website or blog. A preview of your video+player is shown at the right.

Use the small link at the bottom to quickly view in a preview page.
