This article targets customers that still use the Bits on the Run (a.k.a. JW Platform) dashboard, providing more info about the new JW Player dashboard, as well as frequently asked questions.

  • If you are already using the JW Player dashboard, see the Account & Billing topic.
  • If you have not used JW Player before, please read our introduction article.

The New Dashboard

In the new JW Player dashboard, you will notice a brand new design, and several new features including:

  • Deeper Analytics: Including device, domain & country breakdowns.
  • External URLs: Manage, embed & analyze self-hosted (live) streams.
  • Multiple Users: Manage access for more users in admin or editor roles.
  • Player Downloads: Get the self-hosting ZIPs, including our mobile SDKs.
  • Ad Scheduling: Setup midrolls, waterfalls and overlays directly in our GUI.

Moving to our new dashboard will not affect your content delivery, in-progress transcodes, API integrations or billing. Only the user interface will be updated (instantly and seamlessly). Watch this video demo of the dashboard for a helpful walk-through:

Change: New Analytics

The new JW Player dashboard offers a more robust set of analytics versus the JW Platform dashboard. In addition to the aforementioned device/domain/country breakdowns, it includes unique daily viewers, play/completion rates by video and real-time updates of the current day (highlighted by dotted lines).

In order to calculate these analytics, we have built a new data pipeline. Depending upon the way you embed your videos, the new analytics may be significantly different than your old analytics. In short, if you use single-line embed codes all will be fine, but if you play direct MP4/HLS links in a self-hosted JW Player (or 3rd party player), you likely have to do some work. See this analytics article for more details.

Change: Reseller Model

The old JW Platform dashboard offered a multi-account environment we call our Reselling Model. In the new JW Player dashboard, we have updated this model in several ways:

  • We introduced the concepts of separate Users and Properties. This is more flexible versus the old sub-accounts, since users can now be assigned to one or more properties (content libraries).
  • We made available these multiple properties for all Enterprise customers. This means we don't have separate reseller accounts anymore; everybody gets it.
  • We have removed the ability to whitelabel the dashboard through a custom logo, custom CSS and custom dashboard DNS mask, due to limited usage. Note that Contentserver DNS masking remains available.
When a reseller migrates to the new dashboard, all his sub-accounts will be automatically converted to properties. However, secondary user logins will not be transferred over and have to be re-created. Additionally, note that multiple properties are only available to Enterprise users, not Premium or Free.

Frequently Asked

Why is there a new dashboard?

Over the past 2 years, we have worked on integrating the functionalities of JW Player (Cloud player, mobile SDK downloads, etc) with those of JW Platform (hosting, streaming, playlists, resellers, etc) - also known as Bits on the Run, our previous name. This new dashboard is the result of that, essentially offering all features from JW Player and JW Platform under a single roof.

Additionally, the new dashboard was redesigned from the ground up as a single-page JavaScript application, allowing such items as background video uploading, quick publishing and analytics that update in realtime. See our external blog post for more information.

I like the old dashboard. Do I have to Migrate to the new dashboard?

Yes. We will sunset the JW Platform dashboard on April 1st, 2016. The new dashboard retains all existing functionality though, plus adds a host of new features (like deeper analytics, management of external URLs and multiple users, and access to player downloads).

Moving to our new dashboard will not affect your content delivery, in-progress transcodes, API integrations or billing. Only the user interface will be updated (instantly and seamlessly).

I am still on the Pay-as-you-go Billing model. Do I have to migrate to the new billing model?

Yes. We will stop accepting new Pay-as-you-go payments on April 1st, 2016. At that date, we will also sunset the JW Platform dashboard and migrate its users to our new JW Player dashboard.

For typical Pay-as-you-go usage, we now offer the JW Player Premium edition. Priced at $299/year, it includes 50GB storage forever and 250GB streaming per month. Depending upon your usage volumes, this is 2 to 10 times cheaper than your existing Pay-as-you go pricing! On top of that, we offer a 50% discount if you have an outstanding storage or streaming balance.

If you stream and/or store more than the amounts included with JW Player Premium, we will contact you with a custom offer. Alternatively, feel free to contact us for more info.

If I migrate to JW Player Free, can I later upgrade to Premium or Enterprise?

Yes. You can upgrade to JW Player Premium or Enterprise at any time. Do note the 50% Premium discount we offer current Pay-as-you-go customers will expire after April 1, 2016.
