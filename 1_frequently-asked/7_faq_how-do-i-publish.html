Welcome! We're excited to help you get started — JW Player makes it easy to publish a video that will play everywhere. This tutorial will walk you through publishing your first video with the JW Player dashboard.

If you haven't already, be sure to read What is JW Player? to make sure this is the solution you need.

 

STEP 1

Sign Up

If you have already signed up for a JW Player account, and you have also activated your email address, skip to Step 2.

In this example, we'll look at the Free sign-up process. If you'd like to explore a JW Player Premium account or above, visit our pricing page.

Free Sign-Up

To sign up for a Free JW Player account, enter your email address at jwplayer.com/sign-up.

Activate Your Account

Confirm your email address by clicking the "Activate Now" button in the email that we sent you after you signed up in the previous step.

 

STEP 2

Prepare Your Video(s)

Is the content you'd like to publish coming from your computer, or from elsewhere on the internet?

If you've already uploaded your content, skip to Step 3.

Uploading from your computer

If you're adding content from your computer, note where the file is located in your folders.

Navigate to the content list in your JW Player Dashboard.

Click the blue "Add Uploads" button near the top left side of the page.

Select the file (or files) from the filepicker that appers.

As the JW Platform is processing your uploads, you will see a status bar appear at the bottom of your browser window.

Click the blue "details" button in this status bar to view the progress of your individual upload(s).

From this modal you may pause or cancel any or all of your uploads.

As the file uploads complete, the videos will appear in your content list. At this time, they may still be "processing," but you can start adding metadata & more.

 

Adding from the internet

To add content already hosted on the internet into your JW Player content library, get the URL for that content.

Note: The video URL is not the same as the page URL. A video URL will be structured like the below example, ending in a video file extension (like .mp4).

If your video URL does not have a file extension appended, but you are certain that it is an accepted media format, continue with the URL you have. (Read our Media Formats Reference for details on supported file formats.)

Select "add URL" from the dropdown on the blue content button in the content list page.

When you click "Add URLs", a modal will appear. One or more URLs can be entered here — click "+ another" to add more than one.

Paste your URL(s) here, and click "Add" when you are done.

These items will appear in your JW Player content library, and from there you can add metadata & more to each media record.

 

Step 3

Customize Your Player

Each new JW Player account contains two example players: one responsive, and one fixed-width. These players are ready to go! If you don't need to customize a player, skip to Step 5

To get started, you can edit one of these existing players — or you can create a new player by clicking the blue "New Player" button on the left-side of the player list page.

Each player detail page contains options for editing player behavior, design, and functionality. For more about updating these settings, see the articles in our Branding & UX section.

 

Step 4

Embed

Once your content and player are ready, you can click "embed" from anywhere in the dashboard to trigger Publish Mode. Click here to learn more about Publish Mode.

From here, you can swap out the selected player or content — the "change" buttons will bring you to your content or players lists to make a new selection.

In the "embed" section, select the "iFrame" option if you do not have access to your website's <head> code.

Copy the embed code.

Back on your own website in "editing" mode (or logged in as an admin), paste your embed code at the spot in the article or post in which you'd like to place your video.

Depending on which website tool you're using, you'll want to make sure that you're in "source"/"code" view, or in an "embed" editor. If you're building your own website, this embed code goes anywhere you'd like in the <body>.

Then, be sure to save & publish your page, just as you would with any other article or post.

 

Step 5

Track Performance

Once your video is live, you're ready to see how it's doing!

In the JW Player dashboard, navigate to the Analytics section.

Here, you'll be able to see an overview of your top content and overall video activity, view your content performance stats in a list, view performance by domain, and also view real-time data in the "Right Now" section.

Click here to read more about JW Player Analytics.
